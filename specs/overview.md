# Overview

> **Catchphrase:** _Know what happened, where it happened, when it happened._

Sehyo is a network for **what's happening**. Every post is anchored to an event in the world, with three required elements: a **what**, a **where**, and a **when**. The unit isn't a status update from someone you follow — it's a thing that happened.

## The masonry

The defining surface is an **infinitely-scrollable masonry canvas** of posts, similar to how Pinterest and Unsplash present images. Variable-height tiles, multi-column reflow, no pagination. Panning reveals more **breadth**, not just more **history**. A linear chronological feed is explicitly not the product.

**Time is a depth axis, not a scroll axis.** Holding Shift while scrolling (or dragging a dedicated slider — design TBD) moves tiles **toward or away from the viewer**, and that is how chronology is expressed. Pan to browse breadth; push in or pull out to travel through time. The two axes are deliberately separate: you don't lose your place in the canvas when you change _when_ you're looking at.
